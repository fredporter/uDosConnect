# Community Knowledge

**Category Focus**: Working together, mutual support, and building resilient local communities.

## What Belongs Here

Knowledge for:
- **Collaboration** - Working effectively with others
- **Conflict resolution** - Handling disagreements constructively
- **Mutual aid** - Supporting each other's needs
- **Local resilience** - Building strong, self-sufficient communities

## Content Guidelines

### ✅ Include
- **Practical** organizing techniques
- **Consensus-building** approaches
- **Inclusive** practices (consider diverse needs)
- **Conflict** examples and resolutions
- **Failure** stories and lessons learned
- **Scalable** approaches (small to large groups)

### ❌ Exclude
- Political party ideology or partisanship
- Divisive or exclusionary practices
- Authoritarian organizing models
- Cult-like group dynamics
- Manipulation or coercion tactics
- Unsustainable "movement" activism (burnout culture)

## Community Philosophy

**Human-centric community building means**:
- People before ideology
- Local action before global proclamations
- Practical support before performative gestures
- Inclusive processes that welcome all
- Sustainable organizing (no burnout)
- Resilience through diversity
- Trust through transparency

## Community Principles

### 1. Start Local
- Know your neighbors
- Identify shared needs
- Build relationships first
- Take small actions together

### 2. Build Trust
- Show up consistently
- Follow through on commitments
- Share resources fairly
- Communicate openly

### 3. Stay Sustainable
- Rotate leadership
- Prevent burnout
- Celebrate small wins
- Plan for long-term

### 4. Be Inclusive
- Actively invite diverse perspectives
- Accommodate different needs
- Use accessible language and spaces
- Listen more than you speak

## Getting Started

### Individual Level
1. **Introduce yourself** to neighbors
2. **Identify your skills** to share
3. **Attend local meetings** or events
4. **Offer help** to someone nearby

### Small Group Level
1. **Form a study circle** or skill share
2. **Start a tool library** among friends
3. **Organize a community meal**
4. **Create a mutual aid contact list**

### Neighborhood Level
1. **Map community assets** and needs
2. **Establish regular gatherings**
3. **Create shared resources** (garden, workshop, etc.)
4. **Develop emergency response plan**

## Contributing

When adding community knowledge:
1. **Share real examples** - What actually worked?
2. **Include diverse contexts** - Urban, rural, different cultures
3. **Show progression** - Start small, scale up
4. **Acknowledge challenges** - What obstacles to expect?
5. **Link to resources** - Organizations, guides, tools
